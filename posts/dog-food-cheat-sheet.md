I keep forgetting which human foods are fine for dogs and which ones are a phone call to an emergency vet. Carrots and blueberries, fine. Grapes, raisins, xylitol, onions, garlic, macadamia nuts, not fine, and for some of those the margin is small enough that "I think it's okay" is not a good enough answer. The dogs are no help: their position on every food is yes.

So I made a fridge sheet: one page, two columns, sourced from the [ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center](https://www.aspca.org/pet-care/aspca-poison-control/people-foods-avoid-feeding-your-pets) and the [American Kennel Club](https://www.akc.org/expert-advice/nutrition/fruits-vegetables-dogs-can-and-cant-eat/), with the ASPCA poison control number at the bottom. The sheet is [here as a printable page](/dog-food/) and as a [PDF](/dog-food/dog-food-cheat-sheet.pdf).
